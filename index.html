Hi! This is WellSkin.

A responsive site helping you maintain your skin health by offering the following options :)

Self Examination

Checking your body for moles that exhibit warning signs of skin cancer is very important. Skin cancers that are found and removed early tend to be highly treatable, so by performing regular skin checks you are stacking the odds in your favour. Watch this quick video which would help you to self diagnose.

Check your risk level

Many of you tend to go to the doctor even for a small rash or a bite, which increases their workload and also misspend your time and resources. This option provides you a questionnaire consisting of around ten questions which would help you know about your skin health and also determine if you really need to see a dermatologist.

Reminder alerts

Every day, we have many chores to do, doing housework, meetings, phone calls and so on. It is not easy to memorize all and handle all well,so in order not to forget or miss any important things like your doctor appointments, we took help from a digital reminder software to make it helpful for you.

Nearby Dermatologists

Redirecting you to a page for dermatologists near you. Sometimes it is difficult for people to search for a good specialist to treat your ailment. We here offered you an option to find your dermatologist who are well known for their work and are available in a distance not far from the place you reside in.

Blogs

Providing additional and much-required information for skin-cancer patients. When people enter to the world of cancer, hospitals and sickness, mostly they have no idea of what they are going into. We believe that blogs would help and prepare them for what is to come.

Self Help Groups

It’s never easy to adjust to a cancer diagnosis. Everything changes when you hear the words “you have cancer.” It is a time when many people need extra support. No matter who you are, what your treatment plan looks like,or where you’re at in life, it’s always more than okay to reach out for additional support and aid.

Never be ashamed of a scar.

When it rains, look for rainbows. When it's dark, look for stars. It may be hard to find your silver lining during a cancer diagnosis, but it's there. If you look for it, you will find it.

Surviving cancer is anything but easy. Doing it once may be the toughest thing you ever do. For those who’ve done it more than once, you know firsthand that it never gets easier. That’s because every cancer diagnosis is unique in its challenges. Google “surviving cancer” and you’ll likely find this definition: “Continuing to live or exist, especially in the face of hardship.” You often hear cancer described as a "battle" where you have to "fight" to survive.
